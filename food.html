HAINAMESE CHICKEN RICE

Hainanese Chicken Rice is a deceptively simple dish that consists of tender poached chicken served with fragrant rice. The chicken is usually accompanied by a trio of dipping sauces - ginger paste, chili sauce, and dark soy sauce - which add layers of flavor to the dish. This dish originated from the Hainanese immigrants who settled in Singapore and has since become a national favorite.

CHILI CRAB

Seafood enthusiasts should take advantage of Singapore's local cuisine's signature dish, chili crab. A spicy tomato and chili sauce is stir-fried with the crab to appeal to the taste buds. The delicate combination of spicy and peppery tastes elevates this meal to an exceptional eating experience. Often eaten with mantou, a deep-fried bread ideal for mopping up the rich liquid, chili crab is a popular dish.

LAKSA

Popular spicy noodle soup laksa combines Malay and Chinese tastes. Its unique flavor comes from a blend of spices and herbs blended into the aromatic coconut-based soup. Traditionally, rice noodles, bean sprouts, fish cakes, and cockles are served with laksa. Laksa is a popular meal in Singapore because of the pleasing combination of tastes created by this delectable blend of ingredients.

FRIEND KWAY TEOW

Chinese immigrants in Singapore are the original creators of the stir-fried noodle dish known as char kway teow. To make this meal, cook flat rice noodles along with eggs, bean sprouts, Chinese sausage, and prawns. Both residents and tourists love this delectable meal, which is made with crunchy textures from the vegetables and a smokey and somewhat sweet flavor from the noodles.

ROTI PRATA

In Singapore, roti prata, a famous Indian flatbread, has become a mainstay for breakfast and snacks. Before cooking on a heated griddle, a dough mixture is stretched and turned into thin layers. Sweet condensed milk or a savory curry dipping sauce are common accompaniments to roti prata. This meal is a lovely treat because of the flaky and crispy texture of the bread, together with the fragrant curry and sweetness from the milk.
